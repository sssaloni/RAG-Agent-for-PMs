USABILITY TEST REPORT
Study: Workday Mobile App v3.8 — Employee Self-Service Flows
Date: January 2025 | Researcher: UX Research, Ben Harrington
Participants: 8 sessions (5 individual contributors, 3 managers)
Method: Moderated remote usability testing via Zoom + Lookback
Device split: 5 iPhone (iOS 17), 3 Android (Samsung Galaxy, Android 13)
Companies: 4 (Manufacturing, Healthcare, Technology, Retail)

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
STUDY OBJECTIVES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. Identify friction points in the time-off request flow
2. Understand how managers handle approval tasks on mobile
3. Evaluate perceived performance of pay stub access
4. Assess notification preferences and pain points

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
TASK 1: REQUEST 3 DAYS OF VACATION STARTING NEXT MONDAY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Success rate: 5/8 completed without assistance
Average time: 4 minutes 12 seconds
Task failure points:
- Step 7 (calendar date picker): 6 of 8 participants struggled. The date picker renders as a WebView component — pinch/zoom gestures do not work as expected. 3 participants accidentally selected the wrong dates. 2 participants abandoned here.
- Step 9 (balance confirmation screen): 2 participants did not realise this was a confirmation step vs. completion and tried to navigate back.

Observations:
- All 8 participants expected to see their current PTO balance on the first screen of the flow — it appears only at step 4, causing 3 participants to restart or hesitate.
- 7 of 8 participants wanted to see who on their team is already off during their requested dates before submitting. Team calendar visibility is only available after submission in current flow.

Representative quote: "Why do I have to go through all of this before it tells me how many days I have left? That's the first thing I need to know."
— Individual contributor, Manufacturing

Representative quote: "The calendar is basically unusable on my phone. I have fat fingers, what can I say. I ended up submitting for the wrong week and had to cancel and redo it."
— Individual contributor, Healthcare

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
TASK 2: APPROVE A PENDING TIME-OFF REQUEST (MANAGERS ONLY)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Participants: 3 managers
Success rate: 2/3 completed without assistance
Average time: 3 minutes 8 seconds (vs. 45 seconds on desktop — benchmarked)

Navigation path observed:
Home → Menu → My Team → Time Off → Pending Approvals → Select Request → Approve

All 3 participants commented that this navigation was too deep. Manager 2 said: "By the time I get here I forget why I opened the app."

Key finding: All 3 managers stated they routinely delay mobile approvals until they are at a desktop. Manager 1: "If I'm waiting for a coffee I might try, but usually I just leave it for when I'm at my desk." This delays approvals by an average of 4-6 hours per their estimates, which creates downstream issues for employees trying to book travel.

Pending approvals are not surfaced on the home screen in any persistent way. One push notification is sent when the request is submitted; if the manager misses it, there is no re-prompting.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
TASK 3: VIEW YOUR MOST RECENT PAY STUB
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Success rate: 8/8
Average load time recorded: 9.2 seconds (range: 6.8s – 13.4s) on 4G LTE
Average load time on WiFi: 3.1 seconds

5 of 8 participants made a comment during the wait (unprompted):
- "Is this loading?" (3 participants)
- "I wonder if it's broken" (1 participant)
- "Takes forever" (1 participant)

No loading indicator is shown after the initial spinner disappears, which creates a perception of hang/failure. All 8 participants saw a blank white screen for 4-8 seconds before content appeared.

Participants were asked to rate satisfaction with pay stub access: Average 2.1/5.0.
Primary reason cited: load time. Secondary reason: no offline access (4 participants mentioned wanting to check pay stubs when they have no signal, e.g., on a plane).

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
TASK 4: NOTIFICATION PREFERENCES EXPLORATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Method: Discussion, not task-based. Participants shown their notification history for the prior 7 days.

Average notifications received in 7 days (from Workday): 22 (range: 8–41)
Participants who described volume as "too many": 5/8
Participants who had already turned off all Workday push notifications: 2/8 (both cited volume as the reason)

Notification types participants said they WANT to receive:
- Time-off request approved/declined (8/8)
- Action required today (approvals with deadlines) (7/8)
- Open enrollment reminders (5/8)
- Pay stub available (4/8)

Notification types participants said they DON'T want:
- Training reminders for non-required courses (7/8)
- "Your profile is X% complete" (8/8)
- Weekly summary digests (6/8)
- System maintenance notices (8/8)

Representative quote: "I turned them all off because I was getting pinged constantly about completing my learning plan. Now I miss things that actually matter. There's no middle ground."
— Manager, Technology

RECOMMENDATIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Priority 1 (critical): Rebuild date picker as native mobile component. Current WebView implementation is the single biggest friction point across the most-used flow.
Priority 2 (critical): Surface pending manager approvals on home screen with one-tap action. Deep navigation is killing manager adoption of mobile approvals.
Priority 3 (high): Implement skeleton loading states for pay stub. The blank white screen is perceived as a crash. A visible progress state buys tolerance for load time while engineering improves performance.
Priority 4 (high): Redesign notification system. Implement preference centre with granular category controls. Ship a conservative default set (time-off + critical deadlines only) to rebuild trust with users who have opted out entirely.
Priority 5 (medium): Show PTO balance and team calendar on step 1 of the time-off request flow. This is a single data display change with high perceived value.
